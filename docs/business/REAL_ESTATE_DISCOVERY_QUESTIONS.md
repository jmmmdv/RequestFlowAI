# Real estate discovery questions

Practical questions for a realtor, team lead, or broker office owner. You do not need every question
in one meeting — follow the conversation.

**Tip:** Ask for a recent example (“Tell me about the last buyer lead that almost slipped through”)
instead of only yes/no answers.

**Related:** [REAL_ESTATE_PILOT_NOTES.md](REAL_ESTATE_PILOT_NOTES.md) ·
[REAL_ESTATE_DEMO_SCRIPT.md](REAL_ESTATE_DEMO_SCRIPT.md)

---

## Current lead and request process

1. When someone wants to buy or sell, how do they usually contact you first?
2. Who sees new inquiries first — you, an admin, or a shared inbox?
3. What happens between “new message” and “someone is working on it”?
4. Where do you track follow-ups today — CRM, spreadsheet, phone reminders, memory?
5. Walk me through the last lead that came in on a busy Saturday. What happened?

---

## Buyer inquiries

6. How many buyer leads do you get in a typical week?
7. What information do you need before you call a buyer back?
8. How do you decide which buyers get priority?
9. Do buyer leads ever sit unanswered because you were in showings or closings?
10. Where do buyers submit inquiries — website, portal, text, social media?

---

## Seller inquiries

11. How do seller leads usually find you?
12. What is your first step when someone asks “what is my home worth?”
13. How fast do you aim to respond to a potential listing lead?
14. Do you lose seller opportunities when you are mid-transaction on another deal?
15. Who prepares valuation follow-up — you, admin, or a partner agent?

---

## Showing requests

16. How do buyers request showings today?
17. What makes a showing request urgent vs flexible?
18. Have you ever double-booked or missed a showing request buried in email?
19. Does anyone else on your team need visibility into pending showings?
20. Would a single link for “request a showing” help your website or sign-in sheet flow?

---

## Home valuation requests

21. How many valuation or CMA requests do you get per month?
22. Are they mostly serious sellers or early-stage browsers?
23. What is your standard follow-up sequence after a valuation request?
24. How long does it take from request to first human reply?
25. Would an organized queue for valuation leads help you prioritize?

---

## Follow-up process

26. How do you remind yourself to follow up with warm leads?
27. What falls through the cracks most often — buyers, sellers, or past clients?
28. Do you use templates or is every follow-up written from scratch?
29. How do you know when a lead is “cold” vs still worth pursuing?
30. If you were out for two days, could your team see what still needs a reply?

---

## Missed opportunities

31. Can you think of a deal or lead you lost because of slow or missed follow-up?
32. How often does that happen — rarely, monthly, weekly?
33. What did that cost you in time, stress, or commission?
34. What would have prevented it — better inbox, CRM discipline, more staff?
35. Is missed follow-up a top-three problem for you right now?

---

## Current tools and CRM

36. What CRM or tools do you use today?
37. What do you like about them? What do you ignore?
38. Does everyone on the team use the CRM the same way?
39. Is there a gap between “CRM” and “day-to-day messages”?
40. Would you want a lighter intake layer in front of your CRM, or a replacement?

---

## Team workflow

41. How many people touch incoming requests — agents, admin, transaction coordinator?
42. How do you assign a new lead to the right agent?
43. Where do handoffs break down?
44. Do clients ever contact the wrong person and get bounced around?
45. Would a shared request inbox help, or would agents resist it?

---

## Response time

46. What response time do you promise clients, even informally?
47. What is your actual average first response on weekdays? Weekends?
48. Do you measure response time today?
49. Would faster acknowledgment (even “got it, calling by 5pm”) help conversion?
50. What slows you down most — volume, showings, or disorganized intake?

---

## Automation interest

51. If one repetitive step disappeared tomorrow, what would you pick?
52. Would auto-routing by zip code or price range be useful?
53. Are you interested in automatic acknowledgment emails when a form is submitted?
54. What automation have you tried that did not stick?
55. Do you want more automation, or more simplicity?

---

## AI interest

56. Have you tried ChatGPT or other AI for listings, emails, or lead replies?
57. What would you trust AI to **suggest** vs what must stay human-only?
58. Would AI-generated summaries of long buyer emails save you time?
59. Are you concerned about AI making mistakes on pricing or legal wording?
60. Would you pay extra for AI triage once the basics work reliably?

---

## Privacy and data concerns

61. What client information are you uncomfortable putting in a new tool?
62. Do you need clarity on who can see leads inside your office?
63. Are there brokerage or franchise rules about where lead data can live?
64. Do you need a simple privacy notice on public forms?
65. What would make you comfortable running a 30-day pilot with real (or redacted) leads?

---

## Willingness to test a pilot

66. If we set up a simple intake link in under an hour, would you try it for 30 days?
67. Who else would need to agree — broker, partner, admin?
68. Can you commit to checking the inbox daily during the pilot?
69. Would you share honest feedback even if the product is not a fit?
70. What would make you say no to a pilot right now?

---

## Willingness to pay

71. If this saved you one missed lead per quarter, what would that be worth?
72. Do you pay for tools per agent, per office, or personally?
73. Is USD 0 pilot with feedback acceptable if setup is light?
74. Would USD 99 one-time feel reasonable for a focused 30-day trial?
75. At what monthly price would “request inbox + triage” become a no-brainer vs nice-to-have?

---

## Closing the discovery call

76. What is the single biggest intake problem you want solved?
77. Who should be in the demo — you, admin, team lead?
78. Can we schedule 20 minutes to walk through a live example next week?
79. May I send a one-page pilot summary after this call?
80. Is there anything I did not ask that matters for how you handle leads?
