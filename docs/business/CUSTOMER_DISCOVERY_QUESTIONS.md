# Customer discovery questions

Use these in casual conversations with business owners and operations managers. You do not need to
ask every question in one sitting — pick what fits the flow.

**Tip:** Listen for stories (“last week we lost a client email”) more than yes/no answers.

**Related:** [PILOT_VALIDATION_PLAN.md](PILOT_VALIDATION_PLAN.md) · [PILOT_OFFER.md](PILOT_OFFER.md)

---

## Current request handling process

1. When a client or teammate needs something, how do they usually reach you? (email, phone, form, text, etc.)
2. Who sees new requests first?
3. How do you turn a message into something the team actually works on?
4. Where do you track status today — inbox, spreadsheet, sticky notes, another tool?
5. Walk me through what happened with the last urgent request you got.

---

## Pain points

6. What is most frustrating about how requests come in today?
7. Where do things fall through the cracks?
8. How often do you discover a request days or weeks late?
9. What does that cost you — lost revenue, angry client, overtime, stress?
10. If you could fix one thing about request handling, what would it be?

---

## Volume of requests

11. Roughly how many client or internal requests do you get per week? Per month?
12. Are there busy seasons or spikes?
13. How many are truly urgent vs can wait a few days?
14. How many people on your team touch incoming requests?

---

## Departments involved

15. Which roles handle requests — owner, admin, sales, support, technicians?
16. Does work hand off between people? Where does that break down?
17. Do clients ever contact the wrong person and get bounced around?

---

## Response time problems

18. What response time do clients expect?
19. What response time do you actually hit today?
20. What happens when you are slow to reply?
21. How do you decide what to answer first when everything feels urgent?

---

## Lost / missed requests

22. Have you ever lost a request in email or chat? What happened?
23. How do you know if something was never answered?
24. Do you follow up proactively, or only when the client chases you?

---

## Current tools

25. What tools do you use today? (Gmail, Outlook, WhatsApp, Trello, Asana, Zendesk, etc.)
26. What do you like about them? What do you hate?
27. Have you tried a “proper” ticketing or CRM system? Why did it stick or not stick?
28. Would you replace those tools, or add something lightweight on top?

---

## Willingness to pay

29. If this saved you one missed client request per month, what would that be worth?
30. What do you pay today for software that helps you run the business?
31. Would USD 29–79/month be reasonable if it clearly organized requests and priorities?
32. Who approves software purchases — you, partner, finance?
33. Would you pay more for higher volume or more team seats?

---

## Automation interest

34. What repetitive steps do you do for every new request? (copy/paste, assign person, send ack email)
35. Which of those would you automate first if it were safe and simple?
36. Are there actions you would **not** want automated without human approval?

---

## AI interest

37. When you hear “AI” for request handling, what do you hope it does?
38. Would rule-based priority and category labels be enough to start, if they were accurate enough?
39. What would make you trust an AI suggestion — explainability, edit before send, audit log?
40. Would you pay extra for AI analysis, or expect it included in the base plan?

**Be honest in the conversation:** RequestFlow AI uses **rule-based** analysis today. Paid AI is
planned but **not enabled** until pilots prove demand and margin.

---

## Data / privacy concerns

41. Whose data would flow through the system — clients, employees, both?
42. Any industries or data types you worry about (health, legal, payments)?
43. Do you need a specific country for data storage or a DPA before signing up?
44. Who on your side cares about security — just you, or IT/legal too?
45. What would make you comfortable using a new SaaS tool for client requests?

---

## Closing questions (pilot fit)

46. If we set up a simple intake link and dashboard for 30 days, would you route real requests through it?
47. Can we schedule a 30-minute onboarding call this week?
48. Who else should be in the room for that call?
49. What would make this pilot a win for you?
50. Can we do a short feedback call at the end and, if it went well, use your logo or a quote?
