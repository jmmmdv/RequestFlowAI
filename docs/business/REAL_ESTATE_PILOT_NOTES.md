## Why real estate is a good pilot market

Real estate teams receive a steady mix of inbound leads and follow-up tasks from many channels.
RequestFlowAI is built for exactly that pattern: collect requests in one place, suggest priority,
and turn them into trackable work — without requiring a heavy CRM rollout.

Real estate is a strong pilot market because:

- **High inbound volume** — buyer leads, seller leads, showing requests, and valuation questions arrive daily.
- **Speed matters** — slow follow-up often means a lost commission.
- **Many channels** — email, text, phone, website forms, and referrals create scattered threads.
- **Small teams** — solo agents and small broker offices need simple tools, not enterprise complexity.
- **Clear success signal** — if fewer leads are missed and response time improves, the value is obvious.

---

## Main problems real estate teams may have

| Problem | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Scattered intake | Leads sit in personal inboxes, texts, and voicemail |
| Unclear priority | Urgent seller vs casual browser inquiry treated the same |
| Missed follow-up | Showing requests or valuation asks slip through during busy weekends |
| No shared visibility | Admin or partner cannot see what the agent owes clients |
| Weak handoffs | Buyer lead goes to wrong agent or sits unassigned |
| Status unknown | Client asks “did you get my message?” and the team is unsure |

## Example request types

| Type | Typical source |
|---|---|
| Buyer lead | Website, Zillow/Realtor.com, open house sign-in |
| Seller lead | Referral, yard sign, “thinking of selling” form |
| Home valuation request | Website CTA, past client, neighbor referral |
| Showing request | Buyer agent email, weekend open house follow-up |
| Investor inquiry | Cash buyer, rental portfolio owner |
| Relocation question | Corporate transfer, military PCS, out-of-state move |
| Vendor/referral request | Past client needs inspector, lender, or contractor |
| Urgent client issue | Contract deadline, access problem, last-minute showing |

## What RequestFlowAI can help with today

- **Public intake link** — one branded form for buyer/seller/general inquiries
- **Central request inbox** — see new submissions in one dashboard
- **Rule-based classification** — category, priority suggestion, internal summary, recommended next action
- **Work-item tracking** — move requests through statuses (backlog → in progress → done)
- **Team workspace** — organization with roles for small teams (within current plan limits)
- **Reference numbers** — clients can cite a request ID when following up
- **Audit-friendly trail** — important actions recorded for accountability

Current analysis is **rule-based** (keyword and urgency patterns), not a large language model.

---

## What RequestFlowAI should not promise yet

- Full MLS integration or automatic property matching
- Two-way SMS or WhatsApp ingestion
- Automated drip campaigns or marketing automation
- Commission tracking, transaction management, or e-signature workflows
- AI-written listing descriptions or comparative market analysis (CMA) generation
- White-label mobile apps or per-agent branded sub-portals
- Enterprise brokerage rollouts with custom SSO and compliance packs

Position honestly: **request inbox and triage**, not a replacement for Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, or
your full CRM — though it can sit **in front of** or **beside** those tools during a pilot.

---

## Why paid AI / OpenAI is still disabled

RequestFlowAI deliberately keeps **paid AI off** until:

1. **Customer demand** — pilots confirm that rule-based triage is not enough.
2. **Pricing** — you know what real estate teams will pay for smarter routing.
3. **Gross margin** — API costs per organization stay sustainable on Pro/Business plans.

Rule-based classification is sufficient for early pilots: it surfaces urgency, suggests next steps,
and reduces inbox chaos without per-request API spend. When a customer consistently asks for richer
summaries or natural-language routing, that becomes a validated upgrade path — not a day-one promise.

## What success looks like in a 30-day pilot

| Signal | Target (directional) |
|---|---|
| Adoption | Team submits or receives at least 15–30 real requests through the portal |
| Response time | Median first response improves vs baseline (even informally) |
| Missed leads | Fewer “I never heard back” complaints or lost threads |
| Workflow fit | Agent or admin checks the inbox at least daily during active weeks |
| Honest feedback | 30-minute exit call with specific likes, gaps, and willingness to continue |

**Pilot success is learning**, not a signed enterprise contract. A good outcome is a clear answer:
“Yes, this saves us time” or “No, our CRM already covers this — but we need X instead.”
