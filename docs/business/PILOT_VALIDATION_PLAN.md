# Pilot validation plan

RequestFlow AI is ready for **founder-led pilots** before any paid AI spending. This plan keeps
validation practical: prove that real businesses want request intake, triage, and tracking — not
that the product has every automation feature on day one.

**Related:** [PILOT_OFFER.md](PILOT_OFFER.md) · [CUSTOMER_DISCOVERY_QUESTIONS.md](CUSTOMER_DISCOVERY_QUESTIONS.md) ·
[PRICING.md](../finance/PRICING.md) · [AI cost control summary](../security/AI_COST_CONTROL_IMPLEMENTATION_SUMMARY.md)

---

## Goal of pilot validation

Answer one question with evidence:

> Do small businesses pay (or strongly commit to pay) for a simple place to collect requests, see
> priority, and track follow-up — **before** we turn on OpenAI or scale infrastructure?

Success is not “they liked the demo.” Success is **repeat usage**, **clear pain relief**, and
**willingness to continue at a paid price** after the pilot.

---

## Ideal early customer profile

Best-fit pilots are small teams that already lose requests across channels:

| Trait | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| 5–30 people (or solo with subcontractors) | Big enough to feel pain; small enough to decide fast |
| Service business, agency, consultant, or small IT/support team | Natural fit for client/internal requests |
| Requests from email, forms, phone, or chat | They feel fragmentation today |
| No heavy enterprise IT procurement | You can start in days, not quarters |
| Owner or ops manager can say yes | One decision-maker for the pilot |

**Lower fit for first pilots:** large enterprises, teams that need deep CRM/ERP integration on day
one, or buyers who only want “AI magic” without changing how they handle requests.

---

## What pain RequestFlow AI solves

| Pain | How RequestFlow AI helps today |
|---|---|
| Requests scattered across email and messages | One public intake link and dashboard |
| Unclear priority | Rule-based category, priority suggestion, and next action |
| No single status view | Admin dashboard with request status tracking |
| Follow-up gets dropped | Visible queue; audit trail for important actions |
| Team does not know who owns what | Organization workspace with roles (foundation in place) |

**Today:** analysis is **rule-based**. Paid AI is **disabled**. Pilots should validate workflow
value, not LLM quality.

---

## What to validate before adding paid AI

Do **not** enable OpenAI until pilots prove:

1. **Intake is used** — real requests submitted weekly, not a one-time test.
2. **Dashboard is opened** — someone checks status without you reminding them.
3. **Priority/triage is trusted** — even rule-based labels save time or reduce debate.
4. **Willingness to pay** — customer accepts USD 29–199/month (or pilot fee) after trial.
5. **Volume is known** — you can estimate requests/month and analyses/month per customer.
6. **Margin story works** — at their volume, plan price covers infra; future AI cost fits inside
   [unit economics](../finance/UNIT_ECONOMICS.md).

If pilots fail on usage but succeed on interest, fix onboarding and workflow before adding variable
AI cost.

## 30-day pilot structure

| Week | Focus | Your actions | Customer actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1 — Setup** | Go live on one intake link | Create org, configure slug, 30-min onboarding call | Share link with real clients or internal team |
| **Week 2 — First real traffic** | First 10+ requests | Check dashboard with them; fix blockers | Route at least one real channel through RequestFlow AI |
| **Week 3 — Habit** | Weekly review | 15-min check-in: what’s urgent, what’s stuck | Update statuses; note what’s missing |
| **Week 4 — Decision** | Pay / continue / stop | Present pricing; capture testimonial or structured feedback | Say yes to paid plan, extended pilot, or honest no |

**Cadence:** one onboarding call, two short check-ins, one closing call.

---

## Success criteria

Treat the pilot as **successful** if most of these are true:

- [ ] At least **20 real requests** submitted in 30 days (adjust down only for very low-volume niches)
- [ ] Customer opens dashboard **at least weekly** without you chasing them
- [ ] Customer says RequestFlow AI **reduced missed requests or confusion** (specific example)
- [ ] Customer agrees to **paid plan**, **paid pilot extension**, or **strong testimonial + referral**
- [ ] You learned **actual monthly request volume** and team size for pricing
- [ ] No critical blocker (privacy, workflow, trust) that you cannot fix in 60 days

---

## Red flags

Stop or pause the pilot conversation if you see:

| Red flag | What it usually means |
|---|---|
| “We’ll try it someday” with no link shared | No real pain or no champion |
| Zero requests after week 2 | Wrong channel, wrong user, or wrong problem |
| “We need full Salesforce integration first” | Enterprise sale; not MVP pilot |
| Only interest in AI buzzwords | May churn when they learn analysis is rule-based today |
| Refuses any feedback call | You will not learn; do not extend free forever |
| Data/security concerns with no path to resolve | Legal or IT block; document and defer |
| Expects 24/7 white-glove support for USD 0 | Misaligned expectations — reset with [PILOT_OFFER.md](PILOT_OFFER.md) |

---

## Decision after pilot: continue, pivot, or stop

| Outcome | Signals | Next step |
|---|---|---|
| **Continue** | Usage + willingness to pay + clear ROI story | Convert to Starter/Growth/Business; document case study |
| **Pivot** | Interest in problem, wrong packaging or missing one workflow | Change intake flow, pricing tier, or vertical focus; run second 30-day pilot |
| **Stop** | No usage, no payment intent, or unit economics impossible | Do not add paid AI or scale AWS; archive learnings in discovery notes |

**Rule of thumb:** after **3 pilots**, you should have a clear continue/pivot/stop signal. If all
three stall on usage, fix the product or positioning before spending on AI APIs.
